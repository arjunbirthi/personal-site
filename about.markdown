I'm an engineer, writer, and builder, in no particular order — currently in Seattle, working on distributed systems by trade. I also think seriously about AI safety, particularly the gap between where technical safety research is happening and where a robust governance regime would need to extend.

My broader interests run through psychoanalytic theory, political thought, and continental and dharmic philosophy. What draws me to all of them are questions about selfhood, alienation, and what technology reveals about the human condition.

I believe these questions are central to my technical work. I find it impossible to understand how technology — including AI — is affecting us without some account of who we are. And you can't build anything worth building without asking who it's for and who controls it.

More concretely: what would it mean to build technology that distributes power rather than concentrates it? I'm drawn to questions of data ownership, decentralized infrastructure, worker cooperatives in tech, and new models for internet governance.

I write about these things, among others, on this site. If any of this connects with what you're working on or thinking about, I'd love to hear from you. Criticism and disagreement are as welcome as encouragement and agreement, and I'm always on the lookout for people working on similar problems. Feel free to reach out to me at arjunbirthi.work@gmail.com.

- Arjun Birthi
