Hi! I'm Arjun Birthi (he/him). I'm a Seattle-based engineer, writer, and builder. I work on distributed systems and think seriously about AI safety, particularly the gap between where technical safety research is happening and where a robust governance regime would need to reach.

My broader interests run through continental philosophy, psychoanalytic theory, and political thought. I'm working through Lacan, Marx, Hegel, and Žižek and finding them essential for thinking about technology and power. I'm drawn to questions about selfhood, alienation, desire, and what technology reveals about the human condition. I believe this is linked to my technical work: I think you can't understand what AI is doing to us without some account of who we are, and you can't build technology worth building without asking who it's for and who controls it.

That question takes a concrete form for me: what would it mean to use technology for democratic rather than concentrating purposes? From there, I find myself drawn to questions of data ownership, decentralized infrastructure, worker cooperatives in tech, and rethinking how the internet is governed and by whom.

I write about these things on this site. If any of this connects with what you're working on or thinking about, I'd love to hear from you. Criticism and disagreement are as welcome as agreement. I'm especially always on the look-out for people who are working on similar problems. Feel free to reach out to me at arjunbirthi.work@gmail.com.
